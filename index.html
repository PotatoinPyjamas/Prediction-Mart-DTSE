Our mission

PredictionMart brings transparent, verifiable markets for future events — from macroeconomics to technology launches and geopolitical outcomes — enabling institutions and curious individuals across Asia to discover collective probability, hedge risk, and monetize foresight.

Why prediction markets?

Collective intelligence

Markets aggregate distributed information: prices encode a crowd’s belief about an event's likelihood.

Transparent payoff

Each share pays a fixed amount if the event occurs — the current price equals the market-implied probability (e.g., $0.42 ≈ 42%).

How price maps to probability — quick primer

If a contract pays $1 if "Event X happens", and a share trades at $0.30, the market's consensus probability is 30%. Buying shares is equivalent to betting that the event will occur; selling is effectively betting it won't. Liquidity and market mechanics determine slippage and execution price.

Example transactions

Example 1 — Product launch (short-term)

Market: "Will Company X release Product Y by Nov 30, 2025?" Price when Anna buys: $0.25. Anna spends $100 to buy 400 shares. If the product ships by Nov 30, each share pays $1 → Anna receives $400 (profit $300 minus fees). If not, she loses her $100 stake.

Example 2 — Macro (hedge)

Market: "Will Country Z's CPI exceed 6% in Q4 2026?" Price: $0.60. Ravi believes the risk is lower and sells 200 shares (collects $120). If CPI stays ≤6% (event does not occur), Ravi keeps $120 as profit. If it exceeds 6%, he owes $200 (net loss $80).

Example 3 — Speculative play

Market: "Will Project A get regulatory approval by June 2026?" Price drifts from $0.40 to $0.70. Mei bought at $0.40 and sold at $0.68 after news — she realized a 70% gain before resolution.

Market integrity & operations

PredictionMart only lists events with verifiable resolution sources and a clear resolution clause. We apply KYC/AML in the launch jurisdictions and partner with adjudicators (trusted news sources, regulatory filings) for objective settlement. Liquidity is supported via automated market makers (AMM) and optional professional market-makers under monitored terms.

Risk & compliance summary

We operate under a limited-scope regulatory model out of Dubai or Singapore, and will restrict access by jurisdiction as required. Users should treat PredictionMart as a derivatives-style product — read risk disclosures and limits on our terms page.

Quick FAQ

Q: Are trades settled in local currency?
A: Initially INR & USD support; more options later.

Q: How do you prevent manipulation?
A: Volume and price monitoring, market-maker contracts, and dynamic fee schedules to deter wash trading.

Q: Can I short markets?
A: Yes — selling shares is equivalent to shorting (subject to margin rules).
